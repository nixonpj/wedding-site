## Mehendi

**Time:** 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM  
**Date:** February 26, 2026  
**Location:** Garden Pavilion  
**Duration:** Approximately 2 hours  
**Dress Code:** Casual traditional Indian attire. Yellow, orange, or green colors are preferred.

### What to Expect

The Mehendi ceremony is a joyful pre-wedding ritual where intricate henna designs are applied to the bride's hands and feet. This is a fun, interactive event where family and friends gather to celebrate. Light refreshments and traditional snacks will be served throughout the ceremony.

---

## Sangeet

**Time:** 6:00 PM - 9:00 PM  
**Date:** February 26, 2026  
**Location:** Main Hall  
**Duration:** Approximately 3 hours  
**Dress Code:** Festive Indian attire. Bright colors and traditional outfits are encouraged.

### What to Expect

The Sangeet is an evening of music, dance, and celebration! Family and friends will perform traditional and modern dances. This is a lively, festive event with dinner service. Expect lots of dancing, music, and joyful celebration as we come together to celebrate the upcoming wedding.

---

## Haldi

**Time:** 9:00 AM - 11:00 AM  
**Date:** February 27, 2026  
**Location:** Courtyard  
**Duration:** Approximately 2 hours  
**Dress Code:** Yellow or cream-colored traditional Indian attire. Please avoid wearing your best clothes as turmeric may stain.

### What to Expect

The Haldi ceremony is a traditional pre-wedding ritual where a paste made of turmeric, sandalwood, and other auspicious ingredients is applied to the bride and groom. This ceremony is believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits. It's an intimate family ceremony filled with blessings and laughter. Light breakfast will be served.

---

## Wedding

**Time:** 12:00 PM - 4:00 PM  
**Date:** February 27, 2026  
**Location:** Main Wedding Pavilion  
**Duration:** Approximately 4 hours  
**Dress Code:** Traditional Indian formal attire. For women: sarees, lehengas, or salwar kameez. For men: kurta pajama, sherwani, or traditional dhoti.

### What to Expect

The main wedding ceremony will take place under the beautifully decorated mandap. This is the most significant event where traditional rituals will be performed, followed by the exchange of vows. The ceremony includes various rituals that bring together both families. A traditional lunch will be served after the ceremony. We ask that you refrain from using flash photography during the ceremony as we have professional photographers covering the event.

---
